How You Think

The Cognitive Layer

Before AI can think like you, we need to understand how your mind actually works. Not your niche. Not your offer. The patterns, beliefs, and lens you bring that nobody else has. These questions will feel different from any brand exercise you've done before. That's the point.
What do you believe about your industry, your clients, or the way most people approach your field that almost nobody else is saying out loud?
The thing you've known for a while but haven't said plainly. Don't edit yourself.
When a client comes to you with a problem, what is the thing underneath the problem that you almost always see that they cannot see yet?
The pattern you recognize before they name it.
Finish this honestly.
This is your market position in one breath.
Most people in my space...
↔
I believe...
What is a perspective or approach you held five years ago that you no longer hold? What changed?
Your evolution is part of your positioning. This shows how you think, not just what you know.
Who is the person you are most built to serve? Not their job title. Describe them by where they are in their life or identity right now.
What are they carrying? What have they tried that hasn't worked?
When you are doing your best work, what is actually happening? Not the task. The feeling, the conditions, the kind of person you're serving.
Describe it as a scene, not a job description.

Layer Two of Four

How You Feel

The Emotional Layer

This is the layer most AI tools skip entirely. Identity isn't only a brain. It's feelings too. What you protect, what you will never compromise, and what you want people to feel when they're in your world. This is what makes AI output feel human, not just accurate.
What are the two or three values that are non-negotiable in how you run your business and show up for your clients?
Name them, then tell me what each one actually means in how you operate. Not just the word. The meaning behind it.
What do you want someone to feel in the first 30 seconds of encountering your brand? Not think. Feel.
Think about emotional atmosphere before a single word is read.
Describe what working in alignment feels like for you, versus working out of alignment.
This contrast is important. Don't rush it.
In alignment, I feel...
Out of alignment, I feel...
What is something you will not do in your business, even if it would make money?
Your non-negotiables define your brand as clearly as what you will do.
When your work lands exactly right, what has happened? What did you give, and what did they receive?
This is the transformation you're actually in the business of delivering.
Optional · But worth including if you have them
Have you completed any personality or psychological assessments?
Include the assessment name and your specific result for each one. The more precise you are, the more your Blueprint will reflect how you're actually wired — not a generic version of the type. For example: "MBTI: INFP. Enneagram: 4w5. Grit: high (top 10%). Human Design: Manifesting Generator. Attachment: secure. DISC: I/C. Strengths: Activator, Learner, Achiever." One line per assessment is enough. Leave blank if you don't have them.

How You Communicate

The Expression Layer

Now we bring the thinking and the feeling into language. This is where your voice becomes something AI can actually replicate. Not approximate. Replicate. Your voice is not just tone. It is the behavior of your mind and heart on the page.
Tell me the short version of your story. Not your resume. The moment or season that made you understand exactly why you do this work.
This becomes the thread running through everything your brand says.
Write three to five sentences exactly the way you would say them to a client who is about to give up on themselves. Do not edit. Do not polish. Just write.
This is your rawest voice. The one that comes before you think about how it sounds. That's the voice AI needs to learn.
What words or phrases do you find yourself using constantly that feel distinctly like you?
Type a phrase and press Enter to add it. Add as many as come to mind.
What words, phrases, or tones do people in your industry use that you will never use?
Your refusals are as defining as your preferences. This tells AI what you will never sound like.
Complete this: "I want people to read my words and feel _______, not _______."
Then tell me a little about why that contrast matters to you.

not
Not...
Optional · But this is where the magic happens
Paste a real sample of your writing.
Choose something you wrote without heavy editing — a paragraph from a blog post, a newsletter excerpt, an email, or a caption that felt natural. The ideal sample is at least 100 words. Two or three short pieces from different contexts (teaching, selling, connecting) is even better than one polished one. The Blueprint reads how you actually write, not what you say about how you write. Raw is fine.
